Policies, Procedures & Expectations

Welcome to AW Piano Studio

Thank you for choosing AW Piano Studio for your musical education journey. This handbook contains important information about studio policies, expectations, and procedures to ensure a successful and enjoyable learning experience for all students.

As a classically trained pianist with over 20 years of experience and Royal Conservatory training, I am committed to providing high-quality piano instruction tailored to each student's goals and abilities.

Lesson Policies

Scheduling & Attendance

  • Regular weekly lessons are scheduled at the same time each week
  • Lessons are 30, 45, or 60 minutes depending on student level and needs
  • Students are expected to arrive on time and prepared for their lesson
  • If running late, please call ahead - lessons will still end at the scheduled time

Cancellation Policy

  • 24-hour notice is required for lesson cancellations
  • Make-up lessons will be offered for cancellations with proper notice
  • No make-up lessons for same-day cancellations or no-shows
  • Emergency situations will be considered on a case-by-case basis

Payment & Fees

  • Monthly tuition is due by the first lesson of each month
  • Payment can be made by cash, check, or e-transfer
  • Late payment fee of $10 applies after 7 days
  • Additional fees may apply for recital participation and materials
Payment Methods:
• Cash or check made payable to "Adam Wiraszka"
• E-transfer to: adam.wirasz@gmail.com

Practice Expectations

Regular practice is essential for musical progress. Practice expectations are adjusted based on age and level:

  • Ages 4-6: 10-15 minutes daily
  • Ages 7-9: 20-30 minutes daily
  • Ages 10-12: 30-45 minutes daily
  • Teens & Adults: 45-60 minutes daily

Students should use the provided practice journal to track their daily practice sessions and bring it to each lesson.

Materials & Equipment

Required Materials

  • Music books and sheet music (as assigned)
  • Practice journal (provided)
  • Pencil for marking music
  • Metronome (physical or app)

Instrument Requirements

  • Acoustic piano or quality digital piano with 88 weighted keys
  • Proper piano bench (adjustable height recommended)
  • Good lighting and quiet practice environment

Royal Conservatory of Music (RCM)

Students have the option to participate in the RCM program, which provides:

  • Structured curriculum with clear learning objectives
  • Regular examinations to measure progress
  • Certificates and awards for achievement
  • Preparation for advanced music studies

RCM participation is optional and based on student interest and commitment level.

Recitals & Performance Opportunities

Students are encouraged to participate in performance opportunities, including:

  • Studio recitals (typically held twice per year)
  • Festival competitions
  • Community performances
  • RCM examinations

Performance participation helps build confidence and provides goals for students to work towards.

Communication

Open communication between teacher, student, and parents is encouraged. Please feel free to:

  • Ask questions during lessons
  • Email concerns or questions between lessons
  • Request parent conferences to discuss progress
  • Provide feedback about lesson content or pace

Studio Etiquette

  • Arrive on time and prepared for lessons
  • Bring all required materials to each lesson
  • Practice assignments between lessons
  • Be respectful of the studio and equipment
  • Turn off or silence cell phones during lessons
  • Parents are welcome to observe younger students' lessons

Termination Policy

Either party may terminate lessons with one month's written notice. Students leaving mid-month will receive a prorated refund for unused lessons.
